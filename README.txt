----------------------
 Q U I C K  S T A R T
----------------------


1. Make a song in tortia.it with OpenMPT.

2. Copy the pattern data from your song into tortia.txt

3. Double click tortia.py and click on the big logo.

4. Your assembled .bin is in the "kit" folder, "!THIS_IS_YOUR_SONG!.bin".



There is a guide in the docs folder, however here is a summary of guidelines to keep everything running smoothly:


-  The slocum driver recognizes your song 32 rows at a time, so you must paste at least 32 rows into tortia.txt. Likewise, the total number of rows pasted must be a multiple of 32.

*IF YOU PASTE 293 ROWS INTO TORTIA.TXT, YOU WILL ONLY HEAR YOUR SONG UP TO 288 WHEN YOU CONVERT.


- Your song must be no longer than 512 rows. After 512, the slocum driver loops back to the first row and starts assembling from the beginning again for as many rows that come after 512. Uselessly enough, you can convert up to 800 rows before you run out of memory.


- The "tempo" setting in OpenMPT must remain at 150. You adjust the speed of playback through "Ticks/row:". The ticks/row setting you choose in the tracker for your song should match the ticks/row setting in tortia.py when you convert it.


- Make sure to paste both channels into tortia.txt, like the example that is in there now. Otherwise the conversion will screw up.


- Don't put anything into tortia.txt except OpenMPT pattern data.


- Make sure there are no blank spots in between pasted rows. Use the ModPlug header to connect your pasted patterns.
